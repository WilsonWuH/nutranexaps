Review Frames Choline as a Neurocognitive Nutrient Across the Lifespan

By Nutranexa News | July 22, 2026

A new narrative review calls choline an underused neurocognitive nutrient, linking prenatal intake gaps and limited human trial depth to a need for stronger formulation and clinical planning.

What the Review Argues

The Nutrition Research review describes choline as a central nutrient for neurodevelopment, one-carbon metabolism, and potential lifelong cognitive support.

It highlights fetal and neonatal development as the best-supported window while also reviewing possible roles in neuroprotection and later-life cognition.

Where the Gaps Are

The authors note that preclinical evidence is extensive but human data are still limited in scale and duration, which makes causal conclusions difficult for many outcomes.

They also emphasize that choline intake continues to fall short of recommendations in the United States and elsewhere, especially among pregnant women.

Buyer Takeaway

For ingredient buyers, the review supports continued demand for choline and phospholipid forms in prenatal and cognition-support pipelines, but it does not erase the need for careful endpoint selection.

Formulators should treat the paper as a portfolio-level signal toward better nutrient architecture rather than a shortcut to finished-product claims.
