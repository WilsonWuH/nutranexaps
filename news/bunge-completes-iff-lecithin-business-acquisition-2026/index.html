Bunge Closes IFF Lecithin and Soy Ingredients Deal

By Nutranexa News / July 10, 2026

Bunge says it has closed its acquisition of IFF's soy protein concentrate, lecithin, and crush assets, a move that strengthens scale in protein and phospholipid-adjacent ingredient supply.

What Closed

Bunge announced on March 2, 2026 that it completed the acquisition of International Flavors & Fragrances' soy protein concentrate, lecithin, and soy crush businesses.

The company said the transaction leaves it better positioned to serve customers looking for higher-protein and lecithin ingredient solutions.

Supply-Side Signal

For buyers, the notable point is not just ownership change. The combination of crush, protein, and lecithin assets suggests tighter integration across oilseed processing and downstream specialty ingredient output.

That can affect sourcing leverage, regional availability, and the way suppliers package lecithin supply with other soy-derived ingredients.

Buyer Takeaway

Ingredient teams should watch how portfolio integration changes lead times, specification management, and minimum-order flexibility for lecithin and phospholipid-related materials.

A change in ownership is also a useful trigger to refresh vendor files, approved-site lists, and quality-contact details before the next buying cycle.
