A-GPC Trial Reports Acute Cognitive Lift in Healthy Men

By Nutranexa News / July 15, 2026

A 2024 Nutrients study found that alpha-glycerylphosphorylcholine supplementation improved Stroop-test performance in healthy young men, reinforcing demand for fast-acting choline formats in cognitive products.

What Was Tested

A Nutrients paper indexed on PubMed in December 2024 examined acute alpha-glycerylphosphorylcholine supplementation in healthy men.

A-GPC is a choline-based cognitive ingredient with relevance beyond supplements positioned purely around memory because it also appears in focus and performance-oriented concepts.

What the Study Reported

The PubMed summary says both high-dose and low-dose A-GPC supplementation significantly increased cognitive performance as measured by changes in Stroop total score and Stroop completion time.

That result keeps attention on choline format choice when product teams want a faster-acting cognition story rather than a long-term healthy-aging narrative.

Buyer Takeaway

Buyers following nootropic and active-lifestyle categories should expect continuing demand for ingredients that can be framed around acute-use concepts, but they still need disciplined translation from lab tasks to market claims.

A-GPC's growth also reinforces a broader point for phospholipid-adjacent buyers: form matters, and use-case framing matters just as much.
