Review Says Phospholipid DHA Formats Outperform Standard Fish-Oil Triglycerides

By Nutranexa News | July 22, 2026

A recent review says DHA supplied in phospholipid-rich formats can deliver higher intestinal bioavailability than standard triglyceride fish-oil supplements, while also raising cost and formulation tradeoffs.

What the Review Says

The paper compares omega-3 supplement formats including triglycerides, phospholipids, and ethyl esters, with a specific focus on how chemical form affects digestion, absorption, and tissue deposition.

It argues that DHA position within the lipid molecule matters, and that phospholipid-rich formats such as krill oil can offer better bioavailability than common triglyceride-based fish oil.

Why Lipid Form Matters

The review also notes that ethyl ester formats can be more oxidation-stable but depend more heavily on pancreatic activity and meal context to support absorption.

That makes formulation choice a supply-and-performance decision rather than a simple commodity substitution.

Buyer Takeaway

For phospholipid ingredient buyers, the article reinforces why delivery form still matters in premium cognition, prenatal, and healthy-aging products built around DHA.

Commercial teams should weigh bioavailability arguments against cost-in-use, sourcing limits, oxidation management, and destination-market positioning.
