Infant Formula Trial Finds No Body-Composition Shift From Milk Phospholipid Droplets

By Nutranexa News | July 22, 2026

A controlled infant-formula trial found no measurable body-composition advantage from large milk phospholipid-coated lipid droplets enriched in dairy lipids during the first two years of life.

Trial Design

Healthy term infants were randomized to a concept formula with large, milk phospholipid-coated lipid droplets enriched in dairy lipids or to a conventional control formula, with an exclusively breastfed group used as a reference.

Body composition was followed through infancy and into toddlerhood with air-displacement plethysmography, DXA, and abdominal ultrasonography.

Main Result

The abstract reports no significant differences between the two formula groups for fat mass index standard deviation score or other body-composition trajectories through age two.

The concept formula was described as well tolerated and showed similar safety to the control formula.

Buyer Takeaway

For phospholipid and infant-nutrition suppliers, the study is useful because it tests a structural lipid design directly instead of treating all formula fat systems as interchangeable.

The result is also a reminder that premium lipid architecture does not automatically translate into a simple anthropometric win, so application teams still need endpoint-specific evidence.
